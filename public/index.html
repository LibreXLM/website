For most of human civilization, money was inseparable from the communities that used it. With the rise of capitalism and the expansion of financial systems, however, money became increasingly abstract, reduced to digits on screens. It grew detached from daily life, governed by central banks and global markets.

This detachment had consequences. Money, once rooted in trust and cooperation, turned into a cold mechanism of accumulation and control. Growth of the money supply and the logic of endless profit left individuals increasingly powerless. Money was, in a sense, dehumanized.

Then came Bitcoin. For the first time, a digital asset emerged that could not be inflated at will, that was borderless, transparent, and secured by mathematics rather than institutions. Bitcoin fundamentally changed how we think about money and value: it reintroduced scarcity into the digital realm and showed that trust can be rooted in code rather than centralized authorities.

Building on this shift, the Stellar Network pushes the idea further. Its focus on financial inclusion makes it, in my view, one of the most important infrastructures for the future of the financial system. Stellar enables low-cost, high-speed transfers across borders and gives people everywhere the ability to participate in a global marketplace. This is the reason I chose Stellar as the foundation for my personal trading system.

Personal tokens offer a possible reversal. They allow money to once again carry meaning, identity, and imagination. A token can represent a person, a community, or a shared vision. It re-personalizes exchange and reconnects value to relationships.

The LibreTradingEcosystem is a digital laboratory for these ideas. It embraces volatility, autonomy, and experimentation. By participating, you are not only trading tokens, but also testing what money could become in the 21st century.
